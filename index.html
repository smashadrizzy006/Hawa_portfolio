Skills

Basic Life Support (BLS)

BLS is a fundamental level of emergency care that includes CPR, AED usage, and other basic life-saving skills. It is often the first response to medical emergencies such as cardiac arrest, choking, and other life-threatening conditions.

Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS)

ACLS is a higher level of emergency care that builds upon BLS and includes advanced techniques for managing cardiac arrest, stroke, and other life-threatening conditions. It is often used in hospitals and other healthcare settings.

Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS)

PALS is a specialized level of emergency care that focuses on the unique needs of pediatric patients. It includes advanced techniques for managing respiratory and cardiac emergencies in infants and children.

Trauma Life Support (TLS)

TLS is a level of emergency care that focuses on the management of traumatic injuries such as those resulting from car accidents, falls, and violence. It includes techniques for controlling bleeding, stabilizing fractures, and managing other life-threatening injuries.

Airway management

Airway management is a critical skill in emergency care that involves maintaining a patient's airway and ensuring adequate oxygenation. It includes techniques for intubation, suctioning, and other airway interventions.

Patient assessment and evaluation

Patient assessment and evaluation is a fundamental skill in emergency care that involves assessing a patient's condition, identifying potential injuries or illnesses, and developing a plan of care. It includes techniques for taking vital signs, performing physical exams, and interpreting diagnostic tests.

Emergency vehicle operation

Emergency vehicle operation is a critical skill in emergency care that involves safely and efficiently operating emergency vehicles such as ambulances and fire trucks. It includes techniques for driving in high-speed situations, navigating traffic, and communicating with other emergency responders.

Critical thinking and decision-making

Critical thinking and decision-making are essential skills in emergency care that involve analyzing complex situations, making quick decisions, and prioritizing care. They include techniques for problem-solving, decision-making under pressure, and communicating with other healthcare providers.

Physical stamina and endurance

Physical stamina and endurance are critical skills in emergency care that involve maintaining physical fitness and endurance for long periods of time. They include techniques for staying hydrated, maintaining energy levels, and preventing injury during emergency situations.

Strong communication and interpersonal skills

Strong communication and interpersonal skills are essential skills in emergency care that involve effectively communicating with patients, families, and other healthcare providers. They include techniques for active listening, empathy, and conflict resolution.
